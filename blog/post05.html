A Magical Walk Through Red Gates

by Sari

Hi, it’s Sari.

Guess what? I finally visited Fushimi Inari Taisha in Kyoto! I’ve always wanted to go, but I was actually a little nervous.

With all those torii gates lined up, it feels like you’re stepping into another world. Honestly, it’s a bit scary at first (lol).

But when I finally walked through them, I totally understood why people from overseas say it feels so magical.

It’s Like a Bright Red Tunnel

First of all, those thousands of torii gates are even more vibrant than photos. They seem to go on forever.

A friend from overseas told me, “It feels like stepping into a movie world,” and I totally agree.

Once you step inside, the outside noise disappears and it suddenly becomes quiet. For a moment, I even thought, “Wait… can I get back?” (lol)

But then sunlight filters through the gaps between the gates, and it sparkles beautifully. The mysterious atmosphere makes you forget your fear and just want to keep walking.

Inari Shrine: The Guardian of Food

You know those fox statues everywhere at Fushimi Inari? They have sharp eyes and a slightly intimidating look, so when I meet their gaze, I feel like saying, “Oh, sorry!” (lol)

But foxes are actually messengers of the gods, and Inari is the deity of harvest and food. Thinking about it like that, I suddenly felt a kind of connection.

Maybe the reason we can enjoy delicious rice and see full supermarket shelves is because these foxes are watching over us. I found myself bowing and saying, “Thank you, as always!”

Sari

I don’t try to overthink it when I come here.

I just walk, look up, and let the quiet fill me. That’s enough.

The Stairs Are a Little Adventure

To be honest, climbing to the top is quite challenging. At one point, I almost gave up thinking, “How far does this go?”

But then, a foreign tourist greeted me with a smile and a friendly “Hi!” and that little moment gave me a boost.

Even if I didn’t make it all the way to the top, the pond along the way—Shinike—felt incredibly mystical. It was the perfect balance of thrill and calm for someone like me.

Next Time: Kitsune Udon!

Next time, I want to eat Kitsune Udon before I leave. It has fried tofu, which is the fox’s favorite, so I’m sure it will bring me good luck.

Why don’t you come with me next time? It’s a little scary alone, but I feel like we could reach the top together!
