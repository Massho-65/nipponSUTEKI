The Magic of Mt, Fuji in the New Year 2

by Sari

Hello, I'm Sari. Nice to meet you!

Today, we're talking about Mount Fuji, so I'd like to share something a little different with Kiri.

Rather than the kind of information you'd find in a guidebook, I'd like to share the "daily life" of Mount Fuji—the little secrets and moments that make us Japanese smile.

"Find Mt. Fuji" Game

Did you know that in Tokyo, many people choose their apartment based on whether they can see the tiny triangle of Mt. Fuji from their balcony?
Even a tiny blue dot among the skyscrapers can excite us!

"Mt. Fuji is beautiful today!" some people post photos on social media as if it were a beloved neighbor.
On a clear winter morning, seeing white clouds peeking out from a train window or office building feels like a good omen, a sign that the day will be wonderful.
It's like a double rainbow!

Why Mt. Fuji is Painted in Public Baths

If you visit a traditional Japanese public bath, known as a "Sento," you will likely see a giant, hand-painted mural of Mt. Fuji on the wall behind the main bathtub.

In the past, most people didn't have private bathrooms at home. Going to a Sento was a special treat. Soaking in the warm water while gazing at a massive painting of Mt. Fuji made bathers feel as though they were at a luxury resort. It symbolized ultimate relaxation and "soul refreshment." Even while washing your back, having Mt. Fuji there brings a sense of peace.

Mt. Fuji in Your Pocket

Mount Fuji isn't just a distant landmark; we carry it with us every day. If you have a 1,000 yen bill, take it out and look at the back. There it is!

No matter where we go in the country, having Mt. Fuji in our wallets serves as a constant reminder of home and the heart of Japan.

Sari

Edible Mt. Fuji: A Must-Try for Visitors

While tourists love Mt. Fuji for its perfect symmetry, we always tell our friends: "Don't just look at it—try the sweets!" We love transforming our favorite mountain into delicious treats, especially during the New Year season.

  • Mt. Fuji Chocolates: Featuring strawberry "lava" or white chocolate "snow."
  • Mt. Fuji Curry: An eye-catching dish made with blue rice shaped like the mountain.
  • Mt. Fuji Craft Beer: Brewed using fresh spring water from the base of the mountain.

Whether it’s edible, wearable, or giftable, these creations are our way of saying: "We love Mount Fuji!"
